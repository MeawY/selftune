# Nervous-System Safety Language

This guide defines safe language for the SelfTune nervous-system module.

SelfTune is not a medical device, diagnostic tool, treatment system, supplement protocol, or emergency-support tool. It helps users organize observations and notice patterns over time.

## Core Rule

Do not tell users what is wrong with them.

Help users notice what their body may be communicating through repeated patterns.

## Preferred Framing

Use:

- "This may be worth tracking."
- "This could be useful context."
- "The data may suggest a pattern, but it does not prove a cause."
- "Your body may be working hard to adapt or protect you."
- "Consider discussing this with a qualified professional if it is severe, persistent, worsening, sudden, disruptive, or concerning."
- "Use this as a reflection aid, not a diagnosis."

Avoid:

- "This means you have..."
- "Your nervous system is damaged."
- "This proves autonomic dysfunction."
- "This supplement will fix..."
- "This Garmin metric shows that..."
- "You should start, stop, combine, or change medication, supplements, or treatment."

## Safe Terms

Use these as reflection labels:

- Activated
- Wired
- Steady
- Recovering
- Depleted
- Shut down
- Mixed
- Stress load
- Recovery capacity
- Window of tolerance
- Accelerator and brake

Do not use them as clinical labels.

## Wearable Data Language

Wearable data should be described as context.

Safe:

- "A low Body Battery may be a prompt to review sleep, stress load, food timing, and recovery."
- "A high stress reading can reflect many things, including exercise, excitement, caffeine, heat, conflict, illness, or workload."
- "HRV can be reviewed as a trend, not as proof of health or illness."

Unsafe:

- "Your high stress score means you are anxious."
- "Your low HRV proves poor vagal tone."
- "Your Body Battery shows your nervous system is failing."

## Supplement and Nutrition Language

Supplement and nutrition notes are context, not treatment guidance.

Safe:

- "This supplement timing may be worth tracking alongside sleep, stress, and digestion."
- "Meal timing may be useful context for energy and mood."
- "Bring supplement safety, dosage, interactions, pregnancy-related questions, or medication questions to a qualified professional."

Unsafe:

- "Take this dose."
- "This supplement calms the nervous system."
- "This food cures..."
- "This nutrient deficiency explains your symptoms."

## Red Flags

If a user records severe, sudden, persistent, worsening, disruptive, or concerning symptoms, SelfTune should point toward qualified professional support.

Examples include:

- Chest pain
- Fainting
- Severe dizziness
- Intense palpitations
- Breathing difficulty
- Chronic insomnia
- Severe digestive symptoms
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Symptoms that interfere with daily life

SelfTune should not attempt to triage emergencies.
