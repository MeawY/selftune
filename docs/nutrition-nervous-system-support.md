# Nutrition and Nervous-System Support

This guide explains how to record nutrition and supplement context in SelfTune without turning food into a diagnosis or treatment plan.

Nutrition can influence energy, digestion, sleep, mood, and recovery. SelfTune uses nutrition notes as context for reflection, not as proof that a food or supplement caused an outcome.

## How To Use This Guide

Use these categories to improve daily and weekly notes:

1. What did I eat or take?
2. When did I eat or take it?
3. What else changed that day?
4. What did Garmin show?
5. What did my body feel like?
6. What pattern is worth observing over time?

Avoid using this guide to prescribe supplements, diagnose deficiencies, treat conditions, or make medication decisions.

## Protein and Amino Acid Context

Protein-containing foods provide amino acids that the body uses for many functions, including tissue repair, energy metabolism, and neurotransmitter-related processes.

Examples to track:

- Meat, fish, eggs, soy, dairy, legumes, or other protein sources
- Timing of protein intake
- Whether meals felt satisfying
- Energy and mood after meals
- Evening hunger or late snacking

SelfTune use:

Track whether meal regularity and protein context seem related to energy, steadiness, hunger, motivation, or sleep preparation.

Do not claim:

- Protein cures depression, insomnia, fatigue, nerve damage, or mood disorders
- Tryptophan supplementation is needed
- One meal explains a complex emotional or sleep pattern

## B Vitamin Context

B vitamins are involved in energy metabolism and nervous-system function. In SelfTune, they are treated as nutrition context, not as a cure or protocol.

Examples to track:

- B-vitamin-containing foods, if relevant to your diet
- Supplement use, if already part of your routine
- High-pressure weeks
- Fatigue despite rest
- Feeling jumpy, wired, or easily startled

SelfTune use:

Track whether busy weeks, food patterns, and subjective energy seem connected. Bring persistent fatigue, numbness, tingling, mood changes, or major concerns to a qualified professional.

Do not claim:

- B vitamins treat chronic fatigue syndrome, psychiatric conditions, neuropathy, or sleep disorders
- More is always better
- A supplement is safe or appropriate for everyone

## Mineral and Hydration Context

Minerals and hydration can be useful context for muscle tension, energy, temperature, and perceived recovery.

Examples to track:

- Magnesium, calcium, zinc, iron, electrolytes, or other supplements, if already used
- Hydration
- Salt or electrolyte intake, if relevant
- Muscle tension
- Cold hands or feet
- Feeling "on edge"
- Exercise, sweating, heat, alcohol, or caffeine

SelfTune use:

Track minerals and hydration as part of the background context for tension, recovery, and energy. Do not use SelfTune to decide dosing or combinations.

Do not claim:

- Minerals treat hypertension, cardiovascular disease, osteoporosis, anxiety, insomnia, or other conditions
- Magnesium or calcium automatically "calms" the nervous system
- Cold hands or tension prove a mineral issue

## Gut-Brain Context

Digestion and emotional state often move together in daily life. SelfTune tracks gut context as one layer of nervous-system reflection.

Examples to track:

- Fiber-containing foods
- Fermented foods, if part of your normal diet
- Meal timing
- Appetite
- Bloating
- Constipation
- Diarrhea
- Stomach discomfort before stressful events
- Mood changes alongside digestion changes

SelfTune use:

Track digestion alongside Garmin stress, sleep, supplement notes, and emotional tone. Look for repeated patterns over time.

Do not claim:

- Probiotics cure IBS, depression, infections, or immune problems
- The gut directly explains every mood change
- A single digestive symptom proves a nervous-system state

## Healthy Fats and Antioxidant Context

Healthy fats and antioxidant-rich foods can be recorded as part of overall nutrition context.

Examples to track:

- Omega-3-rich foods or supplements, if already used
- Nuts, seeds, fish, colorful vegetables, fruit, tea, cacao, or other polyphenol-containing foods
- Skin changes
- Physical heaviness
- Recovery after demanding days
- Exercise and soreness

SelfTune use:

Track whether overall nutrition quality, recovery notes, and energy patterns appear related across a week.

Do not claim:

- These foods or supplements cure atherosclerosis, reverse aging, treat metabolic syndrome, or prevent disease
- Antioxidants are a guaranteed stress solution
- One food explains recovery

## Glucose Stability Context

Meal timing and composition may affect perceived energy, sleepiness, irritability, and focus.

Examples to track:

- Skipped meals
- Long gaps between meals
- Heavy late meals
- Post-meal sleepiness
- Afternoon energy crashes
- Irritability when hungry
- Eating vegetables or fiber-rich foods earlier in the meal, if that is part of your normal routine

SelfTune use:

Track energy and mood patterns around meal timing. Use the data to ask better questions, not to create rigid food rules.

Do not claim:

- Meal order treats diabetes, hypoglycemia, or weight conditions
- Blood sugar is the definite cause of mood or energy changes
- Everyone should follow the same eating pattern

## Cellular Energy Context

SelfTune avoids making claims about mitochondrial disease or cellular aging. However, it can track the lived experience of energy availability.

Examples to track:

- Eating enough but still feeling depleted
- Low vitality
- Heavy fatigue
- Sluggishness after meals
- Poor recovery after ordinary tasks
- Digestion and absorption context
- Sleep quality and recovery capacity

SelfTune use:

Track whether energy feels supported by sleep, digestion, food regularity, hydration, movement, and stress load.

Do not claim:

- SelfTune can assess mitochondrial function
- Nutrition logs diagnose cellular energy problems
- A routine reverses aging or treats mitochondrial disease

## Weekly Nutrition Reflection

Useful questions:

- Were meals regular enough to support steady energy?
- Did skipped meals, late meals, or heavy meals appear near energy crashes?
- Did digestion change on high-stress days?
- Did hydration, morning water, or meal timing seem to support steadiness?
- Did supplements change at the same time as caffeine, sleep, workload, or exercise?
- What is one simple nutrition context variable to observe next week?

## Safety Boundaries

SelfTune does not evaluate supplement safety, prescribe dosage, or recommend treatment. Food and supplement notes are context for reflection.

Consider qualified professional support for severe, sudden, persistent, worsening, or concerning symptoms, major dietary restrictions, disordered eating concerns, medication interactions, pregnancy-related questions, chronic digestive symptoms, or supplement safety questions.
